Sept 26-27: the Internet Descends on Washington, DC

The FCC is set on killing net neutrality rules. But if we can convince Congress that the rules matter, they can stop the FCC. And convincing Congress is the *only* way to stop ISP-backed legislation that ends net neutrality forever. This is why we need you to visit your lawmakers in Washington DC, or pressure them locally. Can you?

Come to DC Act locally

Here's the plan:

On September 26-27 Internet users from across the country will converge on Washington, DC. Volunteers are working around the clock to ensure that constituents can meet directly with their members of Congress, which is by far the most effective way to drown out the influence of Big Cable’s lobbyists and campaign contributions. Everyone who comes will be able to attend a training on how to be effective in these meetings, and will be paired with a guide to show you where to go on Capitol Hill. If you can’t make it to DC, join us by getting involved locally.

How to get there:

There are limited travel stipends available for people who want to come to DC to meet with legislators but need support with a plane or train ticket. Apply here. If enough people sign up in certain metro areas, we will charter buses to transport everyone for free or a small donation. If you’re driving or looking for a ride, check the carpool forum.

Where to stay:

We recommend booking a hotel or AirBnB as soon as possible. If you can’t afford one, let us know. There are a limited number of spots available for those traveling from outside of DC to participate.
